Artist Quote

“People have told me that these characters are intensely threatening
or aggressively sexual,” Neri explained. “I don’t see them as hypersexual,
though. They’re a fractured character dealing with hierarchies, power struggles.”
The artist views each yellow-haired, pink-skinned figure — inspired both by
women in her family and the blond bombshells depicted in Pop Art — as a facet
of the same imagined character, which is in part informed by Neri’s own various
identities as a mother, lover, daughter and maker. Barefoot and seated on one
of her father’s solid timeworn chairs, Neri answered T’s Artist’s Questionnaire.
